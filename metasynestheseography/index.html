Mobile application for metasynestheseographic research

Proposition for a new research tool

We are currently developing a method of synestheseography that employs an original AR application for mobile devices. It not only facilitates existing modes of synestheseographic practice, but also offers a first venture into metasynestheseographic studies.

Metasynestheseography

New area of research, necessitated by the disappearance of two anonymous synestheseographers who experienced strange anomalies at the University of Chicago while testing prototypes of the AR app.

Account

(transcribed from a video feed, filmed via mandatory cameras embedded AR testers' clothing)

The synestheseographers (henceforth A and B) arrived on campus simultaneously, unaware of one anothers' identities. Upon opening the application, the individuals found a message of unknown origin directing them to specific floors of the Logan center. Thinking the message was from us, they followed the directive: A went to the 1st story of the building, B to the 5th. Further messages indicated that the rules of synestheseography had changed in this setting. Each floor corresponded to a certain sensation (floor 1 was color, floor 2 was texture, etc). Instead of choosing what sensation to transmit to their counterparts via the application, A and B would limit their attention to the sensation assigned to that floor. Furthermore, the direction of their movement was left to their own volition; rather than follow the directives of their partners, the synestheseographers were told simply to "seek evocative manifestations."

The synestheseographers rapidly discovered that in addition to a sensation, each floor had been assigned a "keyword" corresponding to a certain surface or set of surfaces. It was unclear whether these surfaces were natural to the environment or objects "planted" there by the individuals sending the messages, but when their sense descriptions were transmitted via the application, the synestheseographers received further communcations.

For example: floor 1, assigned the sensation of color, held a turquoise statue. When A touched the statue and transmitted the word "turquoise" to the other synestheseographer, he was sent 2 messages:

1. A new floor assignment
2. A fragment of a picture.

Although unclear what the meaning of the picture was, A entered the elevator, where he was given the sensation-assignment of the next floor via the application. Both synestheseographers continued in such a fashion for some time: traversing floors according to assigned sensations, discovering keywords, receiving pictures, and changing levels via the elevator "portal."

After uncovering numerous keywords, both individuals received messages directing them to the same central location in Logan. Here, A and B met for the first time, and shared the messages they had received with one another. They found that the picture fragments they had each been receiving fit together to form the image of another room in the building. They entered the room, and were assaulted with an overwhelming multitude of sensations. On the walls of the room they discovered various symbols, which, when viewed through the camera function of the synestheseographic research application, revealed bizarre message fragments from a group of unidentified individuals we have since labeled "metasynestheseographers"...
