Investigation into fate of disappeared researchers

The culmination of our trail in Logan will be a super-charged affective space… a ‘tear’ in the Anomal… a qualitative puzzle… an undetermined, intersubjective performance… here, the missing researchers, rendered as pure sense beings within the field of absolute affect, will communicate fragmented messages to the players regarding their disappearance, the intentions of the Secret Society, the nature of the Anomal itself…

As our two players arrive at two separate entrances to Logan, they will begin to locate and follow the path that the missing researchers took through the building. When a player locates one of the hidden objects left by the researchers as a clue, they will observe the object, probing it for a sense message from the researchers, a code for accessing the next object, a sense identity mediated in another sensation. Once the new sense identity is ascertained, the player sends this clue to their partner, who goes off in search of one of the hidden objects that gives off that sense. These clues may come with some information helpful in finding the object in space as well as in sense. If the players, who do not know what one another look like and most likely have not run into one another at all, successfully follow the trail through trust and cooperation, they will finally meet at the site of the disappearance.

At the end of their trails, the players find themselves together in the Room (diagram below).

fig. 2: the Room, with moderation via Skype instead of physical human presence

Here, the players encounter a partial, physical manifestation of a sense-being. It is seated at a table at the rear of the space, opposite the entrance. Its face is masked, and all of its garments are the same color as the wall behind it, creating the impression of a vague humanoid form against a field of flat color. [Note: as seen in fig. 2, we may decide to use a Skype session projected on a third wall instead of an actual person in the room; pending further consideration.]

Offset on the same wall to the rear of the sense-being is a writing surface—a black or white board—upon which are scrawled three simple words:

“Soothing”
“Invigorating”
“Disturbing”

On either side of the room, facing one another, are two tables, arranged symmetrically with identical pairs of items: two laptops, two projectors, two musical keyboards, two speakers.

Each laptop is running a simple slideshow program, filled with a number of colored backgrounds that the players will be able to page through at their will. Each laptop is connected to a projector on the same table, which displays the contents of the screen on the opposite wall. The keyboards work in a similar fashion: each player’s keyboard is connected to a speaker on the other player’s table. Hence, while the players are not facing one another, their manipulations of color and sound affect the immediate environment of their partners.

Before the players begin experimenting with the space, the projectors only display the laptops’ screensavers (the test screen for a television set); the speakers each play a low, droning note. The sense being does not move or speak. No clues are given as to what activity the players must engage in. There are only the words on the surface.

The players’ goal, however, is to cooperatively create 3 affective atmospheres, each corresponding to one of the words written on the surface behind the sense being.

They must employ harmonies/dissonances of color and sound created by the laptops and keyboards to adequately evoke “soothing,” “invigorating” and “disturbing” atmospheres. These atmospheres are silently judged by the sense-being. When it is satisfied with an atmosphere, it gets up from its chair and crosses out the corresponding word on the writing surface. Once the players have achieved every atmosphere, the sense being will put 3 documents on the table in front of it and silently exit the room…
