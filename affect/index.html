Synestheseography and Affect

Recent research opens new terrain

After several months of practice, our senses were finely attuned to the minutiae of everyday space: gravel, shattered glass, discarded plastic, the grain of a wooden railing-- all called out to us like orphan children pleading to be found.

But something happened. While wandering, mapping, stopping to touch, listen, examine, we came across inexplicable surfaces, objects that overcame us, overflowing our senses, our rationality, our intuitive understanding of our own interior life. Vortexes of vertiginous affect, prodding, provoking, gesturing towards something beyond, in excess of our intentions, our expectations, even our desires. Another space? Another realm of feeling?

Our cartographies mutated: scattered trajectories gave way to black holes, bottomless free-falls, high-velocity affective impacts swirling ambiguously through our flesh before finally settling on emotions beyond our control.

Hence, a new synestheseography-- a guided tour of these accidental, visceral landmarks ...

fig. 0: the synestheseography

fig. 1: ".... the silver of the can vibrated with amazing energy. I was first enticed, next overcome by an inexplicable feeling of revulsion. The nausea was so extreme I almost collapsed. I was only able to snap a quick photograph before changing course. Perhaps someone with a stronger stomach will return to the spot and test their sensations against mine..."

fig. 2: "... I dragged my legs through the leaves. The rustling struck something deep within me. Profound nostalgia. Tears welled in my eyes. Half formed images of some childhood I never had flashed in my memory. I paced back and forth through the leaves, silently weeping, for what felt like an eternity. I finally regained my composure and tested a nearby pile of leaves for comparison: no emotion, just the dry, brittle cracking of organic detritus..."

fig. 3: "... My fingers made contact with the rough wood of the railing. Something surged through me. I felt suddenly sovereign, supreme. As if all my inhibitions and self-doubts had melted away in an instant. It was more than confidence: it was power. Elated, I nearly charged up the stairs and into the stranger's home; the barking of a dog sobered my senses and turned me away again..."
