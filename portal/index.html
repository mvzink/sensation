Mobile application for metasynestheseographic research

Proposition for a new research tool

A recent experimental synestheseographic excursion led to the accidental discovery of new terrain in synestheseographic research which we call metasynestheseographic studies. We conceive of a site-specific method of synestheseography assisted by an original AR application for mobile devices, enabling new forms of traditional synestheseography while opening paths into this new research terrain.

The application is described in terms of hypothetical usage based on the actual research which inspired it.

Two synestheseographers arrive at the Logan Center at a predetermined time. Through the application, each synestheseographer is assigned a starting floor. Each floor corresponds to a certain sensation (eg, floor 1 is color, floor 2 is texture). The synestheseographers traverse their respective floors according to the rules of synestheseography, but focus only on the sense that floor has been assigned, transmitting their impressions to the other synestheseographer via the application. Furthermore, unlike in standard synestheseography, in this setting the synestheseographer's direction of movement is not determined by the other synestheseographer's directives, but is a product of their own volition: synestheseographers should naturally be drawn to surfaces that strike them as particularly evocative manifestations of their assigned sensation.

In addition to a sensation, each floor is assigned a "keyword" corresponding to a certain surface or set of surfaces. These surfaces can be either natural to the environment or objects "planted" there by metasynestheseographic researchers. The synestheseographers will not be aware that the keywords exist as they begin the experience, and will never be told what surfaces they are meant to seek out.

For example, floor 1, assigned the sensation of color, will somewhere hold a turquoise object that acts as the key. When the synestheseographer transmits the word "turquoise" to the other synestheseographer, she is sent 2 messages:

1. a nebulous clue pertaining to a location in the building where some message will be conveyed.
2. a new assigned floor.

The synestheseographer then enters the elevator, where she is given the sensation-assignment of the next floor through the application. The elevator hence acts as a portal between both the different levels of the building and the different means of experiencing one's surroundings; each floor is defamiliarized in a different way as the synestheseographers alter their outlook according to one particular sensation.

Once the synestheseographers have each traversed a set number of environments and discovered all the keywords, they are directed to convene at a central location in the building. Here, they meet each other for the first time, and together assemble their respective clues, which direct them to another room in the building.

This room becomes a portal to another space in which the synes­theseographers are assaulted with various simultaneous sensations, and must use the mobile application to decode the environment. Along the walls of the room would be a series of symbols that, when viewed through the camera function of the app, reveal fragments of a message from the metasynestheseographic researchers.
