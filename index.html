Researcher spotlight

The Office for Synestheseography honors two synestheseographers whose hard work has been invaluable

Dr. A

Dr. A is a professor of Art History. His current research interests focus on the cartographies of sensation and spatial embodiments of affect. His doctoral work on post-détente Soviet street art led him to his involvement with synestheseography. He has spent the past year working with the Office of Synestheseography in close collaboration with Dr. B, of the Department of Neuroscience.

(Update: Dr. A will be on leave for the foreseeable future.)

Selected publications:

  • Space Oddities: Mapping Areas of Affective Anomalies [forthcoming]
  • “Can You Show Me Where It Hurts?”: the Locative Reification of Trauma
  • Paint it Red: the Use of Color and Line in Graffiti Across the Iron Curtain

Dr. B

Dr. B is an expert in the neurobiology of perceptual anomalies. Her research interests include the neuronal mechanisms of perception, idiopathic adventitious synesthesia, and the influence of sensory stimuli on emotion. She is currently taking a leave of absence from her research fellowship with the Office for Synestheseography.

Selected publications:

  • fMRI Analysis of Tactile-Emotion Synesthesia
  • Associations Between Color and Emotion in Cases of Traumatic Injury to the Fusiform Gyrus
