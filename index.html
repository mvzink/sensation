Synestheseography

A polemic

The customary experience of space rests on destinations and trajectories, landmarks and end-points.

The senses are dulled, the mind refuses to think the body's intuitions.

Only stress, shock, and duress return the backdrop to center stage; we avoid at all costs this costly interference...

Inner Life, Outer Sensation: Two sides of an artificial split, the product of motion without feeling, space without content, life without life's blood.

With this, a directive:

Reroute trajectories.

Submit lines to sensations.

Turn your eyes toward color,

your skin toward texture,

your ears toward clicks, echoes, rustlings and scrapes...

Only another can tilt your compass, remap your desires.

[synesthesia]

Directions

A novella

(in the second-person, present-tense)

You touch a surface,
reach out to another,
describe a sensation: "I'm touching something red."

You await direction.

...

She's "heading East, looking for something red"
You head West. Keep heading West.

...

She finds something red;
Touches it, describes a sensation: "I'm touching something soft."

You redirect. Reach out: You're "heading North, looking for something soft,"
And she moves South.

...

You continue, until the lines are muddled

...

until the surfaces speak

...
